Profile Questionnaire

A simple form built using HTML, CSS, and JS to collect user profile information.

Objectives

  • Design an interface with select and entry controls.
  • Attach event listeners to select and entry controls.
  • Get user data from select and entry controls
  • Create/modify functions used by event listeners.

Procedure

  1. Find the Week 6 assignment in your Team (class section) on Replit and click "Start project" to begin.
  2. Imagine a fictional social site built for a community of like-minded individuals with a specific niche interest or hobby, e.g.:
    • Lizard terrarium owners
    • Regional sandwich enthusiasts
    • American hurling athletes
    • Earthworm photographers
    • Anything else you might think of!
  3. Design a profile questionnaire form that suits the interests of this community. Your form should gather the following information:
    1. First and last name.
    2. Birthdate (or a different relevant date, like "lizard adoption day").
    3. At least 3 other pieces of personal information relavant to the community, gathered using at least 2 different types of controls suitable for the format of the information (e.g. radio buttons, checkboxes, text entry, etc.).
    4. A color scheme that complements the community's interests.
  4. Building your interface in a flexbox is recommended but not required. However, your form should be designed to work on both desktop and mobile displays by using a media query.
  5. Once the information has been gathered, it should be used to populate a profile bio section elsewhere on the page. This section should also include a default avatar image. Again, the color scheme and styling of this section should reflect the interest's of the community.
  6. Remember to click the green "Run" button as needed to refresh the browser preview update on the right side of the window.

Submission

  1. Click the "Submit" button in the upper-right corner when you are finished. This will notify the instructor of your submission time. You may click "Resubmit" as many times as you'd like to submit changes/updates - however, keep in mind that this will also change the timestamp on your submission.
  2. Submit (copy-paste) the URL of your assignment's Repl (from the address bar) to this Canvas assignment. This will ensure that it gets marked off of your Canvas to-do list.

Grading
