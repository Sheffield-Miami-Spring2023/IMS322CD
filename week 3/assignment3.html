Distinguished Navbar

A navbar with hover behaviors for a brand or product page.

Objectives

  • Practice evaluating and imitating styling elements.
  • Incorporate transitions and animations.

Procedure

  1. Find the Week 3 assignment in your Team (class section) on Replit and click "Start project" to begin.
  2. Choose a physical commercial product that you find aesthetically and/or functionally interesting. This product (or the brand) can be current or retro and should have a fairly strong visual identity. Some examples to think about:
    • The original iPod
    • Vintage cameras
    • A retro video game console
    • A piece of furntiture or home appliance
    • A musical instrument
    • A bike, car, or motorcyle
  3. Design a navbar that fits the aesthetic qualities of this product and/or brand with the following characteristics:
    1. Construct the navbar as a flexbox
    2. Create 4 or more navbar links with approprate names (they do not all have to link to different sites)
    3. Put your links in an unordered list inside of the nav element
    4. Give your navbar links a visually distinct hover state with a transition time
    5. Add a logo in the navbar
    6. Include a photo of the product below your navbar that animates onto the page on load
    7. Create a media query that converts the entire layout to a column for narrow displays
  4. Remember to click the green "Run" button as needed to refresh the browser preview update on the right side of the window.

Submission

  1. Click the "Submit" button in the upper-right corner when you are finished. This will notify the instructor of your submission time. You may click "Resubmit" as many times as you'd like to submit changes/updates - however, keep in mind that this will also change the timestamp on your submission.
  2. Submit (copy-paste) the URL of your assignment's Repl (from the address bar) to this Canvas assignment. This will ensure that it gets marked off of your Canvas to-do list.

Grading

  • Grading comments will be added directly within Replit.
  • One point will be deducted for each error, two points for a missing or non-functioning media query.
