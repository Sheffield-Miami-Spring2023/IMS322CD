Profile Filter

Using data-* attributes and conditionals to visually filter results.

Objectives

  • Construct conditional statements.
  • Visually indicate valid vs. invalid results.

Procedure

  1. Find the Week 8 assignment in your Team (class section) on Replit and click "Start project" to begin.
  2. A generic profile search has been created for you with 12 pre-filled profiles. There are inputs for profile criteria fitlering at the top of the page. Your job is to:
    • Decide on a visual style to indicate valid vs. invalid results (e.g. profile card opacity, background color, display or hide, etc.).
    • Add conditionals in JavaScript to apply the valid vs. invalid visual styling based on the provided age and music data-* attributes.
    • Add a third data-* attribute of your choosing that can be evaluated to filter profile card results.
    • Add an appropriate HTML input control and JavaScript conditionals to filter profile results based on this third data-* attribute.
  3. You do not need to change anything in the CSS and do not need to add anything to the HTML beyond your third data-* attribute. No media query is required for this assignment (the layout is already somewhat responsive).
  4. Remember to click the green "Run" button as needed to refresh the browser preview update on the right side of the window.

Submission

  1. Click the "Submit" button in the upper-right corner when you are finished. This will notify the instructor of your submission time. You may click "Resubmit" as many times as you'd like to submit changes/updates - however, keep in mind that this will also change the timestamp on your submission.
  2. Submit (copy-paste) the URL of your assignment's Repl (from the address bar) to this Canvas assignment. This will ensure that it gets marked off of your Canvas to-do list.

Grading
